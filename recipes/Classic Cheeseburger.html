Classic Cheeseburger


Cheeseburger


Classic All-American Cheeseburger with Cheddar Cheese and topped with burger sauce, lettuce, tomatoes, and onion. How to make the best homemade grilled cheeseburger.


Ingredients

  • 80/20 Ground Beef
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Burger Spread Recipe:
    • 1/2 cup Mayonnaise
    • 1/4 cup Ketchup
    • 2 Tablespoons Sweet Pickle Relish
    • 2 teaspoons Sugar
    • 1 teaspoon White Vinegar
  • Cheeseburger Toppings:
    • Medium Cheddar or American Cheese Slices
    • Ripe Tomato Slices
    • Iceberg or Green Leaf Lettuce
    • Thinly Sliced Onion
    • Hamburger Buns

Steps

  1. Form ground beef into round patties, approximately 1/3 to 1/2 lb. each, depending on desired size. Avoid overhandling the beef to maintain juiciness.
  2. Season the patties well with salt and pepper just before grilling to maximize crust formation.
  3. Preheat the grill to high heat to ensure a hot surface for optimal crust formation and browning.
  4. Cook the burgers to desired temperature:
    • 120 degrees F and below: Rare (red/raw in the center)
    • 130 degrees F: Medium-rare (pink and warm)
    • 140 degrees F: Medium (totally pink, starting to dry out)
    • 150 degrees F: Medium-well (grayish pink, much drier)
    • 160 degrees F and above: Well-done (completely gray, very little moisture)
  5. When burgers are almost done cooking, add cheese and let melt for 1-2 minutes.
  6. To make burger spread, mix mayonnaise, ketchup, sweet pickle relish, sugar, and white vinegar in a medium-sized bowl. Cover and refrigerate until ready to use.
  7. After burgers are done cooking, spread burger sauce all over both patties. Layer toppings between patties and cheese. Serve hot.
